Glossary

Deglazing

Deglazing is a cooking technique for removing browned food residue from a pan. The cooked food (usually meat) is removed from the pan and excess fat is poured off. The pan is returned to the stove and a liquid such a stock or wine is poured into the hot pan loosening the food residue. The pan is scraped allowing the browned food residue to be disolved in the liquid, which can be used as the base of a sauce or to flavor a dish.

Roux

A Roux is a thickening agent made by cooking together equal parts flour and fat. It is traditionally made with butter in clasical french cuisine but it can also be made with vegtable oil or animal fat. It is used as a base for sauces and gravies and to thicken soups
